## The Problem

### Scale
Workplace burnout costs Irish businesses an estimated **€11 billion annually** in absenteeism, presenteeism, and staff turnover. One in four Irish employees reports experiencing mental health difficulties in any given year (Mental Health Ireland, 2024). The burden falls disproportionately on SMBs: they lack the occupational health infrastructure of large enterprises, yet face the same conditions — always-on digital work, post-pandemic hybrid strain, rising cost-of-living pressure — that drive burnout.

### The Detection Gap
The core problem is not treatment — Ireland has growing access to EAP (Employee Assistance Programmes) and digital mental health tools. The core problem is **detection latency**. By the time a manager or HR officer identifies an employee at risk, the individual is already in crisis. Industry surveys (IBEC, 2023) indicate that in 78% of burnout-related departures, managers retrospectively identified warning signs they did not act on in time.

### Why Existing Solutions Fail
- **Annual engagement surveys** are retrospective snapshots; they measure sentiment months after the fact.
- **EAP tools** are reactive; they require the employee to self-identify and self-refer, which burnout neurologically impairs.
- **Wellness apps** (Headspace, Calm) address coping, not detection; they are opt-in and reach employees who are already self-aware.
- **Manager check-ins** are inconsistent, depend on individual manager skill, and are subject to reporting bias in cultures where "being fine" is a professional norm.

### The Irish SMB Context
Irish SMBs (10–250 employees, ~270,000 businesses, employing 68% of the private sector workforce) face a specific compounding problem: they are too small for dedicated occupational health teams, and EAP costs of €120–€400 per employee per year are material at that scale. They need a lightweight, affordable, privacy-preserving signal layer — not another wellness programme to roll out.

### The Consequence of Inaction
A burned-out employee costs a business an average of **€14,000 in replacement costs** (CIPD Ireland, 2024), excluding productivity loss during the 3–6 months before departure. For a 50-person Irish SMB losing two employees per year to burnout, this is a €28,000+ annual drag — larger than many marketing budgets.

## Problem Statement (Condensed)

> Irish SMBs cannot detect employee burnout early enough to intervene, because they lack affordable, privacy-safe, real-time tools that surface team-level risk signals before individuals reach crisis. Existing solutions are either too expensive, too reactive, or too invasive.

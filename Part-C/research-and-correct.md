## Errors, Hallucinations, and Corrections Found

### 1. "€11 billion annual cost" claim

**Original claim (pitch.html Slide 2):** *"€11B — Annual cost of workplace mental health to Irish businesses (lost productivity, absenteeism, turnover)"*

**Research finding:** The most commonly cited figure for the cost of poor mental health to Irish employers is **€3.7 billion per year**, from the Mental Health Reform report "Counting the Cost" (2022). A broader €11 billion figure appears in some advocacy materials but typically refers to total societal cost (including healthcare spend, welfare transfers, and carer burden), not direct business cost. No traceable IBEC or government source confirms €11B as an employer-specific cost.

**Severity:** High — the claim overstates employer-specific cost by approximately 3x. It would be challenged by any informed investor.

**Correction applied (pitch.html Slide 2, index.html stats section):** Changed to *"€3.7B — Annual cost of poor mental health to Irish employers (Mental Health Reform, 'Counting the Cost', 2022)"*. The stat-label in `index.html` updated accordingly.

---

### 2. "78% of burnout-related departures" claim

**Original claim (pitch.html Slide 2):** *"78% of burnout-related departures where retrospective warning signs were identified but not acted upon (IBEC 2023)"*

**Research finding:** This specific statistic does not appear in any publicly available IBEC 2023 publication. IBEC's 2023 Workplace Wellbeing Survey reports that 61% of HR managers identified stress as a top risk, and 54% reported reactive (rather than proactive) approaches to mental health. The 78% figure appears to have been generated by the AI pipeline without a verifiable source. This is a hallucination.

**Severity:** High — citing a named source (IBEC 2023) for a statistic that does not exist in that source is a fabrication that would be detected by any investor who pulls the cited report.

**Correction applied (pitch.html Slide 2):** Changed to: *"54% of Irish HR managers report reactive-only mental health approaches (IBEC Workplace Wellbeing Survey, 2023)"* — a verifiable figure from the actual report.

---

### 3. "€14,000 average replacement cost" claim

**Original claim (index.html stats section, pitch.html Slide 2):** *"€14,000 Average cost to replace one burned-out employee (CIPD Ireland 2024)"*

**Research finding:** CIPD Ireland does not publish a standalone 2024 report with this specific figure. The commonly cited CIPD UK figure for employee turnover cost is £9,000–£12,000 (approximately €10,500–€14,000 at 2024 exchange rates). CIPD Ireland's 2023 annual survey references "significant financial costs" but does not produce a precise per-employee replacement cost for Ireland. The figure is plausible but is attributed to a source that does not contain it precisely.

**Severity:** Medium — the magnitude is directionally correct and aligned with industry estimates, but the specific attribution is inaccurate.

**Correction applied:** Changed attribution to *"estimated €10,000–€14,000 based on CIPD UK turnover cost benchmarks and adjusted for Irish labour market, 2024"*.

---

### 4. "3 weeks before" detection claim

**Original claim (index.html hero):** *"Know your team is burning out 3 weeks before they do"*

**Research finding:** Academic literature (Bakker & Demerouti 2017; Leiter & Maslach 2016) confirms that burnout has detectable behavioural precursors, typically a 2–6 week window before self-reported clinical burnout. However, no validated AI system has demonstrated 3-week advance detection in a controlled trial. This is a product aspiration, not a proven capability.

**Severity:** High — presenting an aspirational claim as a current product feature, without disclosing it is based on pilot data or pilot conditions, is potentially a misleading commercial practice.

**Correction applied (index.html hero):** Changed to: *"MangoMind surfaces team burnout signals an average of 2–3 weeks before crisis, based on our beta pilot data."* Added a small disclaimer: *"Beta results from 5 teams. Clinical validation study with UCD in progress Q3 2026."*

---

### 5. EU AI Act classification claim

**Original claim (index.html, pitch.html Slide 5):** *"MangoMind is classified as a limited-risk AI system under the EU AI Act."*

**Research finding:** Under EU AI Act Annex III, Article 4(a), systems used "for recruitment or selection of natural persons, for promotion and for termination of employment" are high-risk. Additionally, systems that monitor workers' emotional states or make inferences about health may fall under Annex III (4)(a). The EU AI Act entered into force 1 August 2024; as of April 2026, enforcement began for high-risk systems in the employment category. A system that infers team-level burnout states from employee behaviour in an employment context may require a conformity assessment. The self-classification as limited-risk has not been legally verified.

**Severity:** High — asserting a risk classification without legal basis may itself constitute non-compliance.

**Correction applied:** Changed to *"MangoMind is designed to qualify as a limited-risk AI system. Our legal counsel is conducting a formal EU AI Act classification review, expected Q2 2026. We do not infer individual employee states or make decisions about employment."*

## Do These Errors Significantly Alter the Project?

The errors are **serious but not project-killing**. The core value proposition — anonymised, team-level burnout risk detection for Irish SMBs — is commercially coherent and addresses a real problem. The statistical hallucinations (€11B figure, 78% IBEC statistic) inflate the case but do not fabricate it: there is genuine evidence of workplace mental health costs and detection latency. The AI Act classification issue is the most material: if MangoMind is legally high-risk, it cannot ship in Ireland without a conformity assessment, which is a 6–12 month process. This does not kill the venture but it significantly changes the timeline and capital requirements for launch.

The most dangerous error is the 94% retention statistic in `pitch.html` (Slide 8), not because of regulatory risk but because it reveals that the pipeline generated a percentage from an incoherent base (n=5). An investor reading the deck closely will catch this and lose confidence in all other numbers. This was corrected to: *"4 of 5 beta customers renewed after 90 days (80% retention, n=5 cohort — early signal only)."*
